## 2. MACRO VIEW

* **Autonomous agentic networks are rewriting payment plumbing.** Financial networks are moving from human-initiated settlement to machine-to-machine transactions, as demonstrated by [Mastercard](https://www.mastercard.com) launching Agent Pay for Machines and [Ripple](https://ripple.com) deploying an AI-payment toolkit on the [XRP Ledger](https://xrpl.org). This shift compresses transaction latency and requires settlement rails that operate without manual intervention.
* **Onchain private credit networks are scaling via strategic legacy acquisitions.** [Figure](https://www.figure.com) is acquiring residential mortgage lender [Kiavi](https://www.kiavi.com) for $717 million to port originations directly onchain. This transaction establishes a highly liquid, capital-light tokenised private credit network, bypassing traditional securitisation bottlenecks.
* **Central bank plumbing is adapting to atomic settlement on external distributed ledgers.** The [Bank of England](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk)'s synchronisation working group is focusing on linking the UK Real-Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) system to external distributed ledgers. This work is essential to enable atomic delivery-versus-payment (DvP) across incompatible financial networks.
* **Institutional asset tokenisation is transitioning to mainstream capital markets.** [Securitize](https://securitize.io) is approaching a public listing on the NYSE, validating the commercial maturity of onchain asset issuance and bridging tokenised assets directly to public equity markets. This transition forces traditional custodians to adapt to digital ledger architectures.
* **Wealth managers are prioritising stablecoins and tokenisation over speculative crypto assets.** Allocators are pivoting to the operational efficiency of stablecoins and real-world asset (RWA) tokenisation, transforming the back end of commerce through platforms like [Visa](https://www.visa.com). This shift redirects capital from speculative tokens to productive ledger-based financial instruments.
* **European monetary authorities are accelerating sovereign digital euro infrastructure to protect domestic payment rails.** The [European Central Bank](https://www.ecb.europa.eu) and the [Deutsche Bundesbank](https://www.bundesbank.de) are advancing the digital euro to secure monetary sovereignty and complement physical cash against private payment networks.
* **Regulators are moving from enforcement to explicit legal classification for digital assets.** Japan is advancing legislation to tax and classify cryptocurrencies as traditional financial instruments, while the [New York Department of Financial Services](https://www.dfs.ny.gov) is harmonising stablecoin rules with the federal [GENIUS Act](https://www.congress.gov/search?q=GENIUS+Act). This reduces compliance friction for cross-border institutional capital.

## 2. CORE PILLAR DEVELOPMENTS

* **Banking Infrastructure & Commercial Rails:** 
  Commercial payment giants and central banking systems are transitioning to automated, ledger-agnostic rails that compress settlement windows and enable autonomous agentic transaction flows. [Visa](https://www.visa.com) is expanding its AI and tokenisation initiatives, utilising stablecoins to reshape backend merchant and B2B settlement infrastructures. To support autonomous, machine-to-machine stablecoin transactions without manual human oversight, [Mastercard](https://www.mastercard.com) unveiled its "Agent Pay for Machines" framework. Concurrently, [Ripple](https://ripple.com) deployed an agentic payments development toolkit on the [XRP Ledger](https://xrpl.org) to facilitate autonomous transaction execution and settlement. On the public infrastructure side, the [Bank of England](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk) convened its [Synchronisation Thematic Engagement Working Group](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk) to synchronise the UK RTGS system with external ledgers, prioritising atomic delivery-versus-payment (DvP) capabilities.

* **Institutional Asset Management & RWAs:** 
  The migration of illiquid private assets onto programmable ledgers, paired with regulatory endorsements for wholesale tokenisation, is creating a more liquid, scalable environment for institutional capital distribution. [Figure](https://www.figure.com) agreed to acquire residential mortgage lender [Kiavi](https://www.kiavi.com) for $717 million to scale its capital-light, onchain tokenised private credit network. [Securitize](https://securitize.io) is progressing toward a public listing on the NYSE, indicating institutional-grade RWA platforms are successfully accessing traditional capital market structures. In Europe, the [Financial Conduct Authority](https://www.fca.org.uk) and the [Bank of England](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk) issued a joint vision document detailing the scaling of digital tokenisation across UK wholesale financial markets. Furthermore, research from [Bitwise](https://bitwiseinvestments.com) indicates that financial advisors and wealth managers are prioritising stablecoins and tokenisation over speculative digital assets.

* **Sovereign Infrastructure & CBDCs:** 
  Central banks are advancing digital euro and regional wholesale ledger architectures to secure monetary sovereignty while engineering highly interoperable cross-border networks. [Deutsche Bundesbank](https://www.bundesbank.de) Executive Board Member Burkhard Balz positioned the [Digital Euro](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/euro/html/index.en.html) as a necessary sovereign complement to physical cash. [European Central Bank](https://www.ecb.europa.eu) Executive Board Member Piero Cipollone updated the European Parliament on evolving public money systems to maintain European payment sovereignty. Internationally, the [Hong Kong Monetary Authority](https://www.hkma.gov.hk) highlighted cross-border wholesale CBDC achievements, focusing specifically on the deployment of [Project mBridge](https://www.bis.org/about/bisih/topics/cbdc/mcbdc_mbridge.htm). Locally, the [Bank of England](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk)'s [CBDC Academic Advisory Group](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk) published risk-modelling evaluations for a potential digital pound design.

* **Regulatory & Legal Frameworks:** 
  Legislatures and regulators are replacing ambiguous enforcement with clear asset-class taxonomies and uniform stablecoin reserve standards to reduce operational risks for regulated institutions. Japan's upper parliament house is advancing a bill to formally classify and tax crypto assets under the same legal regime as traditional equities. In the United States, the [New York Department of Financial Services](https://www.dfs.ny.gov) proposed revised rules for US dollar-backed stablecoins to coordinate local compliance with the federal [GENIUS Act](https://www.congress.gov/search?q=GENIUS+Act). In Europe, [European Central Bank](https://www.ecb.europa.eu) Executive Board Member Isabel Schnabel compared stablecoin run-risks to money market funds (MMFs) and demanded strict regulatory oversight of reserve asset backing.

## 3. STRUCTURAL & OPERATIONAL PAIN POINTS

* **Interoperability Silos:** Legacy Real-Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) systems remain disconnected from decentralised, external permissionless and permissioned ledgers. Bridging legacy synchronisation mechanisms to multiple disparate blockchain rails introduces significant operational risk and engineering overhead for global banks.
* **Balance Sheet & Liquidity Friction:** Stablecoins and tokenised assets lack uniform federal reserve standards, leading to capital fragmentation across different issuers. The alignment of state regulations (such as the NYDFS) with federal frameworks (such as the GENIUS Act) remains unresolved, limiting the fluid deployment of capital across jurisdictional lines.
* **Post-Trade Plumbing Constraints:** Current custodian and settlement architectures are not optimised for high-frequency, autonomous agentic or machine-to-machine transactions. The absence of automated, real-time risk-mitigation tools for machine-driven payments creates settlement bottlenecks and collateral-management friction.

## 4. NEW HIGH-SIGNAL TARGETS FOR TRACKING

* [Synchronisation Thematic Engagement Working Group](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk): The Bank of England's technical group developing the interface between the UK RTGS and external distributed ledgers.
* [Project mBridge](https://www.bis.org/about/bisih/topics/cbdc/mcbdc_mbridge.htm): The multi-lateral cross-border wholesale CBDC platform developed by the BIS Innovation Hub and participating central banks.
* [GENIUS Act](https://www.congress.gov/search?q=GENIUS+Act): The proposed US federal stablecoin framework influencing state-level New York Department of Financial Services (NYDFS) regulatory compliance.
* [CBDC Academic Advisory Group](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk): The Bank of England's risk-modelling committee evaluating the structural, economic, and security designs for the digital pound.
* [Agent Pay for Machines](https://www.mastercard.com): Mastercard's new automated payment protocol designed to settle high-frequency, autonomous stablecoin transactions between AI agents.
