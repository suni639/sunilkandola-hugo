## 1. MACRO VIEW

* **US banking giants are consolidating interbank settlement utilities under a single tokenised deposit network operated by The Clearing House (TCH).** The private operator, owned by 21 major financial institutions including [JPMorgan, Citi, and Bank of America](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/us-banks-tap-the-clearing-house-for-tokenized-deposit-network/), plans to transition tokenised commercial bank liabilities from single-institution silos into a unified 24/7 interbank settlement system. This infrastructure will directly compete with emerging mid-market options such as the [Cari Network](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/us-banks-tap-the-clearing-house-for-tokenized-deposit-network/) and [Hazel Network](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/us-banks-tap-the-clearing-house-for-tokenized-deposit-network/).
* **The mBridge wholesale CBDC corridor is expanding its Asian footprint with Macau officially commencing live cross-border transactions.** Eleven commercial banks in Macau have integrated with the distributed ledger platform, expanding a network that has processed the equivalent of [$55.49 billion in transactions](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/macau-goes-live-on-mbridge-cross-border-cbdc-platform/) with over 95 per cent of volume settled in tokenised [digital yuan (e-CNY)](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/macau-goes-live-on-mbridge-cross-border-cbdc-platform/). This expansion solidifies the Chinese-developed DLT platform as the dominant cross-border multi-CBDC utility, ahead of competing trials like the [BIS Innovation Hub's Project Agorá](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/macau-goes-live-on-mbridge-cross-border-cbdc-platform/).
* **Goldman Sachs is scaling its GS DAP platform to host blockchain-native real estate funds, bypassing traditional registry systems.** Investment manager LRC Group is launching an on-chain real estate fund utilising the [GS DAP platform](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/tokenized-real-estate-fund-issued-on-goldman-sachs-distributed-ledger/), with [Archax](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/tokenized-real-estate-fund-issued-on-goldman-sachs-distributed-ledger/) providing digital security custody. Interoperability between participants is orchestrated by [Ownera's routing layer](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/tokenized-real-estate-fund-issued-on-goldman-sachs-distributed-ledger/), intentionally sidestepping the security vulnerabilities associated with public blockchain bridges.
* **The European Central Bank is fast-tracking legislative and regulatory frameworks to prevent private stablecoins from monopolising digital settlement.** ECB Executive Board member [Piero Cipollone](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/ecb-highlights-need-for-eu-tokenized-deposit-regulation-to-avoid-fragmentation/) warned that failure to deploy a wholesale central bank digital currency (wCBDC) settlement asset will fragment the European market or force reliance on private stablecoins. Concurrently, the European Parliament's ECON committee will vote on [digital euro legislation](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/digital-euro-in-parliamentary-vote-this-month/) on 23 June 2026, targeting a mid-2027 pilot.
* **Hong Kong is formalising a high-level institutional coalition to standardise legal and regulatory structures for digital bond issuances.** The Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA) has launched a [21-member expert group](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/jp-morgan-hsbc-stanchart-in-hong-kong-tokenized-bond-expert-group/) including HSBC, JPMorgan, and Standard Chartered to scale the market following its landmark HK$10 billion sovereign issuance. To support this, the [Central Moneymarkets Unit (CMU)](https://www.bis.org/review/r260604b.htm) is developing a dedicated Digital Asset Platform later this year for digital bond settlement.
* **US state regulators are actively resisting federal overreach by the Treasury regarding the oversight of state-chartered stablecoin issuers.** The Conference of State Bank Supervisors (CSBS) submitted formal objections to the [US Treasury's proposed rules](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/state-regulators-object-to-treasury-imposing-occ-stablecoin-rules-on-states/) under the [GENIUS Act](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/state-regulators-object-to-treasury-imposing-occ-stablecoin-rules-on-states/), claiming that forcing state issuers to comply with the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) rules exceeds statutory authority and restricts state-level regulatory sandbox flexibility.

## 2. CORE PILLAR DEVELOPMENTS
* **Banking Infrastructure & Commercial Rails:** The Clearing House (TCH) is establishing an interbank [tokenised deposit network](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/us-banks-tap-the-clearing-house-for-tokenized-deposit-network/) for US commercial banks, aiming to unify liquidity pools across institutions like Wells Fargo and Barclays. In parallel, Visa has executed a proof-of-concept on the [Canton Network](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/visa-tests-private-stablecoin-settlement-on-canton-with-brale/) utilising Brale's SBC stablecoin to evaluate private, high-throughput institutional settlement rails and atomic payment flows, distinct from public [USDCx transactions](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/visa-tests-private-stablecoin-settlement-on-canton-with-brale/).
* **Institutional Asset Management & RWAs:** Goldman Sachs has facilitated the issuance of LRC Group's blockchain-native real estate fund on the [GS DAP platform](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/tokenized-real-estate-fund-issued-on-goldman-sachs-distributed-ledger/), utilising a conventional Luxembourg alternative investment fund manager (AIFM) structure via Apex Group. Custody of the digital securities is managed by [Archax](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/tokenized-real-estate-fund-issued-on-goldman-sachs-distributed-ledger/). Meanwhile, the [Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA)](https://www.bis.org/review/r260604b.htm) is preparing its Central Moneymarkets Unit (CMU) to deploy a Digital Asset Platform to expand wholesale digital bond issuance, supported by the [Digital Bond Grant Scheme](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/jp-morgan-hsbc-stanchart-in-hong-kong-tokenized-bond-expert-group/) to offset issuer costs.
* **Sovereign Infrastructure & CBDCs:** Macau has progressed to live operations on the [mBridge platform](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/macau-goes-live-on-mbridge-cross-border-cbdc-platform/), enabling 11 commercial banks to conduct cross-border wholesale CBDC settlement. According to the [Atlantic Council CBDC Tracker](atlantic_council_cbdc_tracker_2026-23), global central bank focus is shifting rapidly toward wholesale DLT utilities—such as the ECB's [Project Pontes](atlantic_council_cbdc_tracker_2026-23), Brazil's [Drex](atlantic_council_cbdc_tracker_2026-23) pilot, and the [New York Fed's Project Agorá](atlantic_council_cbdc_tracker_2026-23)—while advanced jurisdictions (including Canada and Australia) are actively deprioritising retail CBDC initiatives.
* **Regulatory & Legal Frameworks:** The European Parliament’s ECON committee has scheduled a definitive vote on [digital euro draft legislation](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/digital-euro-in-parliamentary-vote-this-month/) for 23 June 2026, laying the legal groundwork for a mid-2027 pilot. In the US, the [Conference of State Bank Supervisors (CSBS)](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/state-regulators-object-to-treasury-imposing-occ-stablecoin-rules-on-states/) has formally challenged the US Treasury's attempt to impose OCC standards on state-chartered trusts under the [GENIUS Act](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/state-regulators-object-to-treasury-imposing-occ-stablecoin-rules-on-states/). Additionally, the [Crypto Council for Innovation (CCI)](https://www.theblock.co/post/403829/crypto-council-for-innovation-launches-coalition-to-push-for-regulatory-clarity-on-vaults) has formed the Vault Coalition, alongside Galaxy and Morpho, to lobby for explicit regulatory standards governing institutional smart contract vault receipts.

## 3. STRUCTURAL & OPERATIONAL PAIN POINTS
* **Interoperability Silos:** The proliferation of private, single-institution blockchain networks continues to fragment liquidity, as highlighted by the [ECB's concerns over tokenised deposit market fragmentation](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/ecb-highlights-need-for-eu-tokenized-deposit-regulation-to-avoid-fragmentation/). To bypass the systemic security vulnerabilities of traditional cross-chain bridges, emerging RWA issuances, such as LRC Group's fund on [GS DAP](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/tokenized-real-estate-fund-issued-on-goldman-sachs-distributed-ledger/), are forced to rely on external, conventional routing layers like [Ownera](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/tokenized-real-estate-fund-issued-on-goldman-sachs-distributed-ledger/) rather than native on-chain interoperability protocols.
* **Balance Sheet & Liquidity Friction:** Commercial banks face capital efficiency constraints and disintermediation risks if digital fiat solutions migrate entirely to private stablecoins. As outlined by the ECB's [Piero Cipollone](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/ecb-highlights-need-for-eu-tokenized-deposit-regulation-to-avoid-fragmentation/), without an active wholesale central bank digital currency (wCBDC) to serve as a risk-free settlement asset, commercial banks cannot safely settle tokenised liabilities without incurring substantial counterparty and credit risk.
* **Post-Trade Plumbing Constraints:** Cross-border wholesale CBDC platforms face operational friction from a lack of regulatory harmonisation and liquidity coordination. While platforms like [mBridge](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/macau-goes-live-on-mbridge-cross-border-cbdc-platform/) demonstrate high transaction volume, central banks remain highly reluctant to allow their sovereign currencies to circulate on third-party platforms. This friction is driving alternative architecture designs, such as [Project Agorá](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/macau-goes-live-on-mbridge-cross-border-cbdc-platform/), where central banks retain unilateral ledger control.
